Israel animal spying

There are conspiracy theories that suggest Israel employs animals for espionage or attacks. These are often linked with theories about Zionism. Some examples include shark attacks in Egypt in 2010, Hezbollah's claims of "spying" eagles, and the discovery of an Israeli-tagged satellite tracking device on a griffon vulture in 2011.

Harold Wilson

Former MI5 officer Peter Wright and Soviet defector Anatoliy Golitsyn have claimed that former British Prime Minister Harold Wilson was a KGB spy. However, historian Christopher Andrew has criticized these claims as unfounded and based on conspiracy theories.
