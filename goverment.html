Political conspiracy theories are often spread through fake news on social media. A 2017 study found that right-wing misinformation is currently more prevalent. These theories may range from wars and international bodies to localized beliefs such as the conspiracy theory about the 118th Battalion controlling local politics in Kitchener, Ontario after World War I.

Crisis actors

Conspiracy theories suggest that mass shootings and other traumatic events are staged, with the help of covert crisis actors who portray victims and their families. Crisis actors are individuals who act as disaster victims in training exercises for first responders.

Illuminati in Europe

Conspiracy theories about the Illuminati, an Enlightenment-era secret society, emerged in the late 19th century when some conservatives believed they were responsible for the French Revolution. In the 1960s, a group of American pranksters known as the Discordians spread hoaxes about the Illuminati, including fake letters to Playboy.
